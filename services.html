Strategic Planning Packages

The Success Track

8th, 9th, and 10th Grades

Success in high-stakes STEM programs and independent college life does not happen by accident. It requires a deliberate, multi-year strategy. This phase is for proactive families who refuse to just check boxes and instead demand a high school experience that is both fulfilling and purposeful. We do not just align your student's natural strengths with an academic path. We build the self-advocacy and technical rigor required to survive and thrive in competitive environments. This is the essential preparation for the student who wants to turn their unique way of thinking into an undeniable professional advantage.

What's Included

  • Strength Discovery: Targeted assessments to align academic paths with natural interests.
  • Long-Term Mapping: A four-year "big picture" plan to keep long-term goals in focus.
  • Scholarship Oversight: Rigorous tracking for Georgia HOPE and Zell Miller requirements to eliminate surprises.
  • Intentional Extracurricular Mapping: Evaluating activities to identify summer and school-year opportunities that build a cohesive story.
  • Preliminary College Criteria: Identifying the size, culture, and location factors that matter most for the future.
  • Empowerment Meetings: Regular sessions to help the student take the lead in their high school journey.

The Outcomes

  • A Clear Roadmap: A customized four-year plan that removes the "what do we do next?" anxiety from the dinner table.
  • Student Ownership: Students who understand why they are taking certain classes, leading to higher engagement and better performance.
  • Optimized Profiles: A balanced resume of activities that feels authentic to the student rather than "manufactured" for admissions.

The Comprehensive Package

11th and 12th Grades

The transition from high school to a high-stakes university environment requires a move from exploration to execution. This high-intensity engagement is for juniors and seniors who refuse to leave their future to chance. It is built for STEM applicants and students with learning differences who must articulate their unique strengths while navigating the complex departmental requirements of modern universities. We do not just submit applications. We ensure every student meets rigorous professional standards and masters the self-advocacy required for college independence. This is the final, strategic move to turn a student's way of thinking into a permanent professional advantage.

What's Included

  • Data-Driven List Curation: Identifying "best fit" schools that balance reach, possible, and likely outcomes.
  • IDEA Assessment to assess college readiness.
  • Full Application Management: A master calendar for all deadlines, removing administrative stress from the home.
  • Intentional Extracurricular Mapping: Refining the presentation of leadership and activities to showcase maximum impact.
  • Personal Statement and Essay Advising: Brainstorming and refining a unique voice for a standout application.
  • Letter of Recommendation Strategy: Assisting the student in approaching the right mentors for impactful endorsements.
  • Interview Preparation: Mock sessions to build confidence in articulating goals and stories.
  • Financial Aid and Enrollment Analysis: A professional second opinion when weighing final offers and award letters.

The Outcomes

  • A Balanced College List: A strategic range of schools where the student is most likely to thrive academically, socially, and financially.
  • A Standout Narrative: Applications that clearly articulate the student's value, especially in technical or neurodiverse contexts.
  • Confident Self-Advocacy: Students who enter college with the ability to interview, communicate with faculty, and manage high-stakes deadlines independently.

How to Get Started

  1. Submit an Inquiry: Fill out the brief form on our Contact page to share your student's current grade and primary goals.
  2. The Intro Session: If it looks like a potential match, we will schedule a 30-minute introductory session (virtual or in person) to discuss your student's needs and see if it is a good mutual fit for your family.
  3. Secure Your Spot: Once we determine the right track for your journey, we will begin mapping out your student's roadmap to success.
